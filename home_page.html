What is Engineering?

Engineering is the ability to apply knowledge and solve multifaceted problems that affect society in order to increase efficiency or innovation. Therefore, representing a crucial component to daily life . Very simple tasks performed on a daily basis such as checking your cell phone, getting your garage door to open, turning your car on, walking onto the 7th floor on a building, broadcasting the weather for the following days, and many more are made available because of the work of engineers. Without engineering, humanity would still be in caves. This is the digital age, so why not pursue this field while it is on the rise.

What is Mechanical Engineering?

Definition:

Mechanical Engineering is a branch of engineering concerned primarily with the industrial application of mechanics and with the production of tools, machinery, and their products.

Impacts to Society:

Mechanical Engineering plays an important role in enhancing safety, economic vitality, and overall quality of life throughout the world.

Examples:

Automobile
 The invention of the automobile had narrowed the gap between rural and urban life. Motor vehicles have significantly changed city life by accelerating the outward expansion of population into the suburbs, allowing people to live in low density residential areas even farther from the inner city.
Water Filtration and Chlorination Systems
 Advancements in clean water technologies was a significant public health intervention of the 20th Century. Before the invention, waterborne diseases counted for nearly a quarter of reported infectious disease deaths in major cities.. The introduction of water filtration and chlorination systems led to major reductions in mortality.
