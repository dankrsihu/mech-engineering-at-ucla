What careers does a degree in Mechanical Engineering offer?

Mechanical engineering is a very broad degree that can lead to distinct careers, of course, all within the branches of engineering.There are by no means a restriction of the careers someone can pursue with such degree, As a matter of fact, because the skills and knowledge that Mechanical Engineers acquire, the possibilities are limitless.

Possible Careers

Mechanical Engineering

  • For example, a degree in Mechanical Engineering brings the option of becoming a Mechanical engineer, where it may be obvious but with this title, mechanical engineers specialize in developing, designing and perfecting mechanical devices(Best Master’s). They work to design power-producing machines such as electric generators, internal combustion engines, and steam and gas turbines, as well as power-using machines such as refrigeration and air-conditioning systems(BLS). This can open doors up in the Aerospace Industry, Oil and Gas Industry, and many more.

    Industrial Engineering

  • The second available option is an Industrial Engineer. With this title, the primary goal is to maximize efficiency(Best Master’s). They use their mathematical and mechanical engineering expertise to solve problems that arise in production or manufacturing of products (Best Master’s). Some possible localities for this application of knowledge would be in transportation or even healthcare. If engineering in healthcare, engineers use scientific engineering methodology to create solutions to complex health care problems and improve the quality of life(Mayo).
    • More info about Industrial Engineering
    Automotive Engineering
  • Thirdly, is Automotive Engineering. This careers yields to engineers with a particular interest in developing car parts, safety technologies, fuel efficient engines or anything else along those lines of innovation, according to The Best Master’s Degree page. Automotive Engineering can lead to working with OEM( Original Equipment Manufacturer) automotive company.

    Biomedical/Biotechnology Engineer
  • Another career possibility with a mechanical engineering degree is that of Biomedical Engineer/Biotechnology Engineer. This field combines both the healthcare aspect as well as the engineering aspect as a hybrid to create methods to construct new medical devices, software or related technologies for use in the healthcare sector(Best Master’s).

    HVAC Engineer

  • A Heating, Ventilation and Air Conditioning (HVAC) Engineer is always an option as well. They are concerned with both the air quality and the temperature in environments where their products are in use(Best Master’s).Their work is emphasized on improving available technologies in heating, ventilation, air conditioning and / or refrigeration systems, as stated in Best Master’s webpage.
